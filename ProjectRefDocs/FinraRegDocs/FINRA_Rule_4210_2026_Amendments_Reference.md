# FINRA Rule 4210 — 2026 Intraday Margin Standards
## Complete Compliance Reference for Broker-Dealer Legal & Compliance Teams

---

**Regulatory Notice:** 26-10 (April 20, 2026)
**SEC Approval:** Release No. 34-105226 (April 14, 2026); File No. SR-FINRA-2025-017
**Federal Register:** 91 FR 20731 (April 17, 2026)
**Effective Date:** June 4, 2026
**Phase-In Deadline:** October 20, 2027

---

## 1. Executive Summary

On April 14, 2026, the SEC approved FINRA's amendments to Rule 4210 (Margin Requirements) under SR-FINRA-2025-017. These amendments eliminate the longstanding "pattern day trader" regime and replace it with new intraday margin standards. The changes affect all FINRA member firms and fundamentally restructure how broker-dealers manage customer margin accounts for intraday trading activity.

Key changes:

- Elimination of the "pattern day trader" designation and all associated requirements
- Removal of the $25,000 minimum equity requirement for pattern day traders
- Elimination of "day-trading buying power" calculations
- Introduction of a new "intraday margin deficit" framework applicable to all margin accounts
- New portfolio margin provisions for accounts under $5 million in equity
- Member firms may choose between real-time monitoring or end-of-day calculation

## 3. Rule Amendments — Structural Overview

The amendments establish the following new provisions under Rule 4210:

- **New paragraphs (a)(17) through (a)(19):** Key definitions (Intraday Margin Level, IML-Reducing Transaction, Intraday Margin Deficit)
- **New paragraph (d)(2):** Core intraday margin requirements
- **New paragraphs (g)(1)(J) and (g)(1)(K):** Portfolio margin updates

The amendments eliminate:

- **Paragraph (f)(8)(B):** Day trading margin requirements (in their entirety)
- **Associated provisions under paragraphs (b), (f)(10), and (g)(13)**
- All interpretations of the former day trading margin requirements

## 4. New Key Definitions

### 4.1 Intraday Margin Level (IML) — Rule 4210(a)(17)

The IML for a customer's margin account at a given time or following an IML-reducing transaction is defined as either:

- **(A)** The amount of cash the customer could withdraw while still maintaining the maintenance margin required under Rule 4210 (excluding paragraph (d)(2)); or
- **(B)** The amount of additional cash (expressed as a negative number) the customer would need to deposit for the account to meet maintenance margin requirements.

### 4.2 IML-Reducing Transaction — Rule 4210(a)(18)

Any transaction that reduces the amount available for the customer to withdraw while meeting the maintenance margin requirement. Examples include:

- Purchase of a security other than to cover a short position
- Short sale of a security
- Short sale of an option

### 4.3 Intraday Margin Deficit — Rule 4210(a)(19)

The highest deficiency following an IML-reducing transaction between the margin required and the equity in the account. This represents the peak shortfall during the trading day.

## 5. Core Operative Provisions — Rule 4210(d)(2)

### 5.1 Requirement to Determine Intraday Margin — (d)(2)(A)

Each FINRA member firm must determine the intraday margin deficit for:

- Each margin account of a customer (excluding good faith accounts and portfolio margin accounts)
- For each day in which there is any IML-reducing transaction

### 5.2 Parameters for Computation — (d)(2)(B)

Members have flexibility in how they compute IML and intraday margin deficits:

**Real-Time Monitoring (Optional):** Members may implement real-time monitoring of customer positions and block transactions that would create or increase intraday margin deficits. This is NOT required.

**End-of-Day Calculation (Alternative):** Members may make a single end-of-day calculation, comparable to the regular maintenance margin computation.

Specific parameters include:

**(i) Sweep Programs:** A member may treat customer deposits at FDIC-insured banks under a Sweep Program (per SEA Rule 15c3-3(j)) operated by the member as a credit balance in the customer's account.

**(ii) Market Value:** Members may use values more recent than the execution price or previous day's closing price to determine current market value, provided the procedure is reasonably designed for that purpose rather than to reduce intraday margin requirements.

**(iii) "As Of" Actions:** Members may allocate "as of" actions either to the approximate time/day during which they are processed or to the earlier time/day recorded for their occurrence.

**(iv) Deposits and Withdrawals:** Members may treat all deposits, withdrawals, and position-closing transactions as occurring simultaneously and immediately after the beginning of the day, regardless of actual timing.

**(v) Multi-Leg Strategies and Options:** Members may treat the following as occurring simultaneously:
- Execution of multiple legs of a spread or strategy with reduced maintenance margin requirement (from a single order submission or substantially contemporaneous execution)
- Creation of a position by assignment/exercise of an option and liquidation of that position on the same day

**(vi) Computing IML When Order Cannot Be Determined:** If two or more activities occurred during a day and the member cannot demonstrate the order, the IML must be computed assuming the activities occurred in the order producing the highest intraday margin deficit.

### 5.3 Satisfaction of Intraday Margin Deficits — (d)(2)(C)

- Intraday margin deficits must be satisfied **as promptly as possible**
- A deficit is "satisfied" when, from end of the deficit day to end of a subsequent day, the customer has made net deposits or otherwise increased the account's IML sufficiently to equal the deficit
- Net deposits or IML increases may satisfy multiple outstanding deficits
- A deficit remains outstanding until satisfied or until close of business on the **15th business day** after the date of the deficit

### 5.4 90-Day Freeze — (d)(2)(D)

If a customer makes a **practice** of failing to satisfy intraday margin deficits promptly AND fails to satisfy a deficit by close of business on the **5th business day** after it occurs, the member must enforce written policies and procedures reasonably designed to prevent the customer from:

- Creating or increasing a short position
- Creating or increasing a debit balance (other than by closing a short position)

This freeze lasts for **90 calendar days** after the 5th business day, or until the deficit is satisfied.

**Safe Harbors — A customer is NOT considered to be making a practice of failing to satisfy deficits if:**

- The deficit does not exceed the lesser of 5% of account equity or $1,000; or
- The member reasonably determines the deficit occurred under extraordinary circumstances

## 6. Portfolio Margin Updates — Rule 4210(g)(1)(J) and (g)(1)(K)

### 6.1 Paragraph (g)(1)(J)

Members must include in their written risk analysis methodology procedures and guidelines for determining and monitoring intraday risk created by activity in each portfolio margin account.

### 6.2 Paragraph (g)(1)(K)

Each portfolio margin account that maintains **less than $5 million in equity** must maintain margin for intraday risk that is substantially similar to the margin the member requires for positions existing at the end of the day.

This preserves the existing $5 million threshold under the portfolio margin provisions.

---

## 7. Net Capital Implications

Under the new framework, if an intraday margin deficit is not satisfied within 5 business days:

- On the 6th business day, members are required to deduct the deficit amount from net capital (per SEC Rule 15c3-1 and, if applicable, FINRA Rule 4110(a))
- This deduction continues until the deficit is satisfied or until the close of business on the 15th business day after the deficit date

---

## 8. Relationship with Regulation T

Regulation T (12 CFR Part 220) and FINRA Rule 4210 operate in tandem:

- **Regulation T** establishes minimum standards for extension of credit at the initial stage of a transaction (initial margin — generally 50% of purchase price for marginable securities)
- **FINRA Rule 4210** imposes ongoing margin and maintenance requirements throughout the life of a position and governs how broker-dealers manage customer credit risk

Broker-dealers must ensure customer margin accounts remain in continuous compliance with BOTH Regulation T and Rule 4210, applying the more stringent requirement where the two overlap.

The 2026 amendments revise FINRA's treatment of intraday and maintenance margin risk. They replace the "pattern day trader" framework with an "intraday margin" model that functions alongside Regulation T's initial margin requirements. Regulation T itself was not amended; the key change is on the FINRA maintenance/intraday margin side.

## 9. Compliance Checklist for Member Firms

### Immediate Actions (By June 4, 2026 or During Phase-In)

1. **Update Written Supervisory Procedures (WSPs)** to reflect new intraday margin standards
2. **Choose monitoring approach:** real-time trade blocking OR end-of-day deficit calculation
3. **Develop/update written policies and procedures** for:
   - Computing IML and intraday margin deficits
   - Sweep Program treatment (if applicable)
   - Market value determination methodology
   - "As of" action allocation
   - Treatment of deposits/withdrawals
   - Multi-leg strategy and options exercise treatment
4. **Establish 90-day freeze enforcement procedures** including:
   - Criteria for determining "practice of failing to satisfy"
   - Safe harbor evaluation process
   - Customer notification protocols
5. **Update customer-facing disclosures and margin agreements**
6. **Remove PDT-related restrictions** from systems and processes
7. **Train staff** on new framework (compliance, margin dept, operations, risk management)
8. **Update technology systems** for intraday margin deficit computation and monitoring
9. **Update net capital computation procedures** for the new deficit deduction framework
10. **Update portfolio margin risk analysis methodology** for accounts under $5M

- Monitor for FINRA's forthcoming interpretive guidance and resources
- Direct questions to idm@finra.org or FINRA staff contacts
- Incorporate findings from the 2026 FINRA Annual Regulatory Oversight Report into compliance programs
