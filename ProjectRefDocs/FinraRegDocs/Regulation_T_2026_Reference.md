# Regulation T (12 CFR Part 220) — 2026 Reference
## Credit by Brokers and Dealers — Interaction with FINRA Rule 4210 Amendments

---

**Authority:** Federal Reserve Board
**Codification:** 12 CFR Part 220
**Current Version:** Updated as of May 2026
**Relationship to FINRA 4210:** Regulation T governs initial margin; Rule 4210 governs ongoing maintenance and intraday margin

---

## 1. Overview

Regulation T is a Federal Reserve Board regulation that governs the extension of credit by brokers and dealers for the purchase of securities on margin. It establishes the framework for initial margin requirements, restricts certain credit transactions, provides enforcement mechanisms, and defines rules for cash accounts.

Regulation T was NOT directly amended by the 2026 FINRA Rule 4210 changes. However, the two frameworks operate in tandem, and broker-dealers must understand how the new FINRA intraday margin standards interact with Reg T's initial margin requirements.

---

## 2. Key Provisions of Regulation T

### 2.1 Initial Margin Requirement

The primary requirement: investors can borrow up to **50% of the purchase price** of a marginable security. This means a customer must deposit at least 50% of the value of securities purchased on margin.

### 2.2 Scope and Application

- Applies to broker-dealers extending credit for securities transactions
- Establishes minimum standards for the extension of credit at the **initial stage** of a transaction
- Does not preclude any exchange, national securities association, or creditor from imposing additional requirements

### 2.3 Margin Requirements Supplement (Section 220.12)

Section 220.12 specifies the required margin for individual securities:

- **Long positions / short puts:** 50% of current market value, or the percentage set by the relevant regulatory authority, whichever is greater
- Specific margin percentages vary by security type and are detailed in the supplement

### 2.4 Key Definitions (Section 220.2)

Terms defined in the Exchange Act or rules thereunder (including Regulation T) carry the same meanings within the FINRA Rule 4210 framework unless otherwise specified.

## 3. How Regulation T Interacts with the 2026 FINRA Rule 4210 Amendments

### 3.1 Complementary Frameworks

| Aspect | Regulation T | FINRA Rule 4210 (as amended 2026) |
|--------|-------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Scope** | Initial credit extension | Ongoing maintenance + intraday margin |
| **When Applied** | At time of transaction | Throughout life of position + intraday |
| **Key Metric** | Initial margin (50%) | Maintenance margin + intraday margin deficit |
| **Amended in 2026?** | No | Yes — new intraday margin framework |
| **Administered By** | Federal Reserve Board | FINRA |

### 3.2 Practical Compliance Requirement

Broker-dealers must ensure customer margin accounts remain in **continuous compliance with BOTH** Regulation T and Rule 4210, applying the **more stringent requirement** where the two overlap.

The 2026 FINRA amendments replace the "pattern day trader" framework with an "intraday margin" model that functions **alongside** Regulation T's initial margin requirements. The intraday margin framework supplements — but does not replace — Reg T initial margin or Rule 4210 maintenance margin.

### 3.3 Extension of Time Filings

FINRA Rule 4230 permits member firms to request additional time to comply with the payment period for purchases and margin deficiencies as required by Regulation T. FINRA publishes annual filing schedules for Reg T, SEA Rule 15c3-3, and Rule 4210 extensions of time.

The 2026 Filing Schedule is available at:
https://www.finra.org/rules-guidance/notices/information-notice-20251107

## 4. 2026 FINRA Annual Regulatory Oversight Report — Margin and Liquidity Guidance

The 2026 FINRA Annual Regulatory Oversight Report (published December 9, 2025) provides compliance insights relevant to margin regulation:

### Key Areas of Focus

- **Liquidity Risk Management:** FINRA expects firms to maintain robust credit policies, set appropriate concentration limits, and conduct ongoing monitoring of customer and counterparty exposures
- **Market Access Rule (SEA Rule 15c3-5):** Firms must maintain pre-trade financial and regulatory risk controls calibrated to their business models, with clear documentation
- **Intra-Day Adjustments:** FINRA expects controls over intra-day adjustments, and notes that overreliance on venue-provided controls without firm-level oversight is inadequate
- **Extended-Hours Trading:** Triggers risk disclosures under Rule 2265, best execution obligations, CAT/TRF reporting, and supervisory processes for lower liquidity environments
- **GenAI and Technology:** Firms using generative AI must maintain oversight, conduct due diligence, and ensure auditability
- **Third-Party Risk:** Firms cannot outsource regulatory obligations through vendor relationships

### Effective Practices Highlighted

- Enterprise-level GenAI oversight with formal review processes
- Model risk management adapted to new technologies
- Embedding conflict checks into supervisory reviews
- Comprehensive post-trade review processes for manipulative activity
- Annual effectiveness reviews of controls and procedures
